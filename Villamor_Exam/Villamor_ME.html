About the Dish

Bicol Express is a creamy stew that combines spice and sweetness in a well-balanced manner. The dish makes use of ginger and local seasonings resulting in a flavorful combination of meat and sauce. Coconut milk adds a mild sweetness to the sharp flavors of chili pepper and bagoong in this Bicol Express recipe. This contrast has created a richness in taste for the dish, contributing to its popularity among Filipino dishes.

Surprisingly, the origin of Bicol Express is credited to Manila, not Bicol. Despite claims from Bicol residents, it is widely accepted that the dish was created in Cely Kalaw’s restaurant in Manila, according to prominent figures in Philippine cooking. In the 1960s, Kalaw decided to present a creamy dish with the slightest punch. After some struggle in entitling the dish, her ears caught the sound of the daily train headed to Bicol. And that was when she got the idea to name it after a train going the Manila-Naga route This recipe aims to be a milder version of Laing, a local dish made from dried taro leaves with rich coconut milk. Bicol Express quickly grew in popularity. And soon, the dish began popping up in several other dining establishments in the country. But what most people didn’t know by then was that the dish did not exactly originate from the region in its own name.

Many Bicolanos assert that a recipe similar to Bicol Express originated in their region. Ginataang Sili, also known as Gulay na Lada, includes balao, a variant of shrimp paste, along with other comparable ingredients such as pork and coconut milk. It has been suggested that Kalaw may have drawn inspiration from Gulay na Lada, particularly to moderate the spiciness of her Laing recipe. Some argue that Kalaw acknowledged drawing inspiration from Bicolano dishes but insist the name and recipe were her own kitchen experiments.



Ingredients
Ingredient Amount
Fresh Pork Belly (sliced into Strips) 500g
Coconut Milk 2 cups
Coconut Cream 2 cups
Siling Labuyo (sliced) 2 pieces
Siling Haba (chopped) 5 pieces
Bagoong - Shrimp Paste 2 tbsp
Onion (chopped) 1 large
Garlic (minced) 5 cloves
Ginger (sliced) 2 thumbs
Fish Sauce 2 tbsp
Water 1 cup
Maggi Magic Sarap to taste
Salt & Pepper to taste
Cooking Oil 2 tbsp

Cooking Equipments to Use

To make Bicol Express, you will need the following cooking tools:

  • Large Pot or Wok: For sautéing and simmering the pork and coconut milk.
  • Knife and Cutting Board: To slice the pork and chop the vegetables.
  • Spatula or Wooden Spoon: For stirring the ingredients during cooking.
  • Measuring Cups and Spoons: To measure the ingredients accurately.

Cooking Instructions
  • Step 1: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  • Step 2: Add the sliced pork belly and cook until browned and tender.
  • Step 3: Stir in the shrimp paste (bagoong) and cook for 2-3 minutes until the flavors meld.
  • Step 4: Pour in the coconut milk, followed by the fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for 50-60 minutes.
  • Step 5: Add water as needed.
  • Step 6: Add the long green chilies and continue to cook for another 5 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  • Step 7: Adjust seasoning like the Salt, Pepper, & Magic Sarap if necessary. Remove from heat.
  • Step 8: Continue simmering until the sauce thickens.
  • Step 9: Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!
