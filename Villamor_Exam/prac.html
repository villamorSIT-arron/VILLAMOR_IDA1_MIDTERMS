What is Bicol Express?

Bicol Express is a creamy, spicy stew made from pork, shrimp, and chili peppers. Originating from the Bicol region of the Philippines, it features coconut milk that balances the heat from the chili peppers and the savory flavor of shrimp paste. Despite its name, Bicol Express is said to have been created in Manila, not in Bicol itself.

The dish's popularity grew in the 1960s when it was first served at Cely Kalaw’s restaurant in Manila. Over time, it became a staple in Filipino homes and restaurants, loved for its rich and spicy flavor.

This recipe combines the heat from long green chilies with the rich sweetness of coconut milk, creating a mouthwatering flavor profile that many find irresistible.

Ingredients
Ingredient Amount
Fresh Pork Belly (sliced) 500g
Coconut Milk 2 cups
Long Green Chilies 10-12 pieces
Shrimp Paste (Bagoong) 2 tbsp
Onion (chopped) 1 large
Garlic (minced) 4 cloves
Ginger (sliced) 2 tbsp
Fish Sauce 2 tbsp
Salt & Pepper to taste
Cooking Oil 2 tbsp

Back to top
Cooking Instructions
  • Step 1: Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  • Step 2: Add the sliced pork belly and cook until browned and tender.
  • Step 3: Stir in the shrimp paste (bagoong) and cook for 2-3 minutes until the flavors meld.
  • Step 4: Pour in the coconut milk, followed by the fish sauce, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  • Step 5: Add the long green chilies and continue to cook for another 5 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  • Step 6: Adjust seasoning if necessary. Remove from heat.
  • Step 7: Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy!
